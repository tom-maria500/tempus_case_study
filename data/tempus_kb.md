## Test Portfolio

### xT CDx — Solid Tumor Comprehensive Panel (Flagship)
- 648 genes (DNA), tumor-normal matched — reduces false positives vs. tumor-only panels
- FDA-approved; CMS ADLT reimbursement at $4,500 (vs. $2,989 for LDT competitors)
- Covers: SNVs, INDELs, CNVs, TMB, MSI, PD-L1, HRD, and all major solid tumor drivers
- Median TAT: 9–11 days from sample receipt
- Sample success rate: >97% for adequate FFPE
- Add-ons: IHC, FISH, MGMT Promoter Methylation
- Best for: First-line profiling in advanced NSCLC, CRC, breast; progression workup; trial enrollment

### xF / xF+ — Liquid Biopsy (ctDNA)
- xF: 105-gene cfDNA panel | xF+: 523-gene cfDNA panel
- Median TAT: 7–9 days from sample receipt
- 9% of patients have unique actionable alterations found in liquid biopsy not detected in tissue alone
- Best for: When tissue is unavailable or delayed; resistance monitoring; serial assessment

### xR — RNA Fusion Panel
- Whole-transcriptome RNA sequencing; FDA 510(k) cleared
- Detects: NTRK, ALK, RET, ROS1, and other clinically relevant fusions
- 21% more patients eligible for fusion-targeted therapies identified with DNA + RNA vs. DNA alone
- No additional tissue required when combined with xT CDx
- Best for: NSCLC and other fusion-driven cancers where DNA alone may miss drivers

### xM — MRD & Monitoring
- **xM tumor-naive (CRC)**: Blood-based MRD for post-surgical colorectal cancer surveillance
- **xM NeXT Personal Dx (tumor-informed)**: Ultra-sensitive; detects ctDNA at 1.67 parts per million; tracks up to 1,800 somatic variants; Medicare covered for Stage II/III breast and Stage I–III NSCLC surveillance
- Best for: Post-surgical recurrence monitoring; therapy response assessment

### xH — Hematology Whole Genome Assay
- First whole genome-based assay in the Tempus portfolio
- Focused on hematologic malignancies (lymphoma, leukemia)
- Launched 2025

### Hereditary Testing (via Ambry Genetics)
- CancerNext®, CancerNext-Expanded® (77-gene panel), BRCAplus®
- Optional +RNAinsight® RNA add-on
- Best for: High-risk patients; germline BRCA; hereditary cancer risk assessment
